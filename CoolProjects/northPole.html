How this Works

This is the North Pole time clock, based on the idea that a single day in the North Pole (time from sunrise to sunrise) is a year elsewhere.
The clock is quite simple actually, and was my first proper experience using JavaScript scripting in a web page. Essentially, the clock takes the current day
and calculates how much time has passed since December 21st (Winter Solstice), of the previous year. Dividing this difference by 15, we can calculate what hour it is
at the pole, in 24hour format. Theoretically, at Summer Soltice in June - the clock should read 12:00 - and 00:00 at Winter Solstice.
The inspiration for this project came from a New Scientist book, entitled "Why Don't Penguin's Feet Freeze" - specifically a question relating to the time in the North Pole.
Albeit very late, hopefully this answers that question quite nicely.
